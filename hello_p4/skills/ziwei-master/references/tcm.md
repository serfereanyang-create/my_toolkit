# TCM Symbolic Foundations

## Open-Source Starting Points

The TCM open-source ecosystem is more fragmented than `iztro`. Use these as references for concepts, data structures, or knowledge-graph patterns, not as clinical authorities:

- `TCMLLM/TCMLLM`: Traditional Chinese Medicine language-model/data project; useful for seeing how TCM text and tasks are structured.
- `ZENGJingqi/GraphAI-for-TCM`: Graph/AI project around TCM knowledge. Useful for knowledge-graph style relationships.
- `WENGSYX/CMKG` and other CMKG-style repositories: Chinese medicine knowledge graph patterns, often covering herbs, formulas, symptoms, and diseases.
- Public herb/formula datasets on GitHub may contain 中药, 方剂, 病证, or symptom relations; inspect source, licensing, and clinical reliability before reuse.

Always verify current repositories and documentation if code, data, or citations are needed.

## Clinical Boundary

Use TCM here as symbolic/traditional reasoning for astrology and divination contexts. Do not diagnose, prescribe, or replace medical care.

Allowed:

- General constitutional tendencies.
- 五行/脏腑/情志 symbolic interpretation.
- Seasonal self-care ideas in broad lifestyle terms.
- Explaining classical concepts for study.

Not allowed:

- Diagnosing a disease.
- Giving herb formulas, dosages, treatment schedules, or acupuncture prescriptions as actionable medical advice.
- Telling the user to stop or change medication.
- Predicting serious illness from a chart.

For real symptoms, advise consulting a qualified clinician.

## Core Correspondences

Five phases:

- 木: liver/gallbladder symbolism, spring, wind, growth, anger, tendons, eyes.
- 火: heart/small intestine symbolism, summer, heat, joy, blood vessels, tongue, spirit.
- 土: spleen/stomach symbolism, late summer/transition, dampness, pensiveness, muscles, mouth.
- 金: lung/large intestine symbolism, autumn, dryness, grief, skin/hair, nose.
- 水: kidney/bladder symbolism, winter, cold, fear, bones, ears, essence.

Use "symbolism" unless discussing verified clinical TCM source material.

Eight principles:

- 阴/阳: interior-rest-cooling-nourishing vs exterior-active-warming-transforming tendencies.
- 表/里: surface vs internal location.
- 寒/热: cold vs heat pattern tendency.
- 虚/实: deficiency vs excess tendency.

Substances:

- 气: movement, transformation, defense, holding.
- 血: nourishment, circulation, grounding.
- 津液: fluids, moistening, metabolism.
- 精: essence, development, reproduction, reserve.
- 神: consciousness, vitality, mental-spiritual expression.

## TCM Layer in Zi Wei Reading

For health-related Zi Wei topics:

1. Start with 疾厄宫 facts: palace, major stars, auxiliaries, 四化, malefics/benefics.
2. Cross-check 命宫, 身宫, 福德宫, and 三方四正.
3. Translate to broad tendencies:
   - 火/铃/羊/陀/七杀/破军 patterns may symbolize acute pressure, inflammation-like imagery, injury-prone or stress-reactive tendencies; avoid literal disease claims.
   - 太阴/天同/天府 patterns may symbolize fluids, digestion, nourishment, rest, damp/cold imagery depending on palace context.
   - 太阳/火象 emphasis may symbolize heat, visibility, circulation, overexertion imagery.
   - 巨门/廉贞 patterns may symbolize hidden stagnation, toxicity, boundary, throat/speech/digestive-symbol layers depending on school.
4. Tie the reading to lifestyle reflection: rhythm, sleep, stress, movement, diet regularity, climate sensitivity.
5. State that actual symptoms override chart symbolism.

## TCM Layer in Yijing Reading

When a Yijing reading asks about health or constitution:

- Use trigram imagery carefully:
  - 乾: head, yang, strength, upward force.
  - 兑: mouth, lung/metal imagery, opening.
  - 离: eyes, heart/fire imagery, clarity, heat.
  - 震: feet, liver/wood imagery, movement, suddenness.
  - 巽: thighs, wind/wood imagery, penetration, subtle change.
  - 坎: ears, kidney/water imagery, cold, danger, depth.
  - 艮: hands/back, stillness, blockage.
  - 坤: abdomen, spleen/earth imagery, receptivity, nourishment.
- Read moving lines as the changing factor rather than a diagnosis.
- If using 六爻, do not infer medical specifics without 用神, 月建, 日辰, 世应, and full line data.

## Seasonal and Five-Phase Advice

Keep advice broad and low-risk:

- Spring/木: gentle movement, planning, stretching, emotional ventilation.
- Summer/火: avoid overexertion, protect sleep, moderate heat.
- Late summer/土: regular meals, reduce overthinking, avoid excess damp/heavy habits.
- Autumn/金: protect dryness, breathing rhythm, letting go.
- Winter/水: rest, warmth, conserve energy, avoid depletion.

Avoid herbs, supplements, and treatment protocols unless framed as non-actionable study material.
